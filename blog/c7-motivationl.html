How does life look through the pleasure, passion and purpose lens

I found this very interesting. Pleasure for me has manifested itself in a big way throughout my life, be it through drinking when I was younger to spending lots of money on material items. I have been aware for a while that this is the case but the chapter in search inside yourself put some context around it. These short term pleasures have invariably left me feeling bad and low afterwards and the initial euphoria soon wears off.
The passion stage was something I found when I was woodworking. When I hit that sweet spot nothing could touch me and I would be steaming along. However quite often something would come along and slow the momentum. I found myself buzzing and thinking about it when I wanted to sleep, however the lows when things weren't working could be quite bad.
Working to a higher purpose is something I have thought about a lot. I believe strongly in equality and social justice but my actions don't match my thoughts. I would like to find a job or a hobby where I made a tangeable difference to peoples lives, not just signing online petitions and ignoring the tough stuff. I am working towards a transition from pleasure to purpose and trying to live mindfully and care about what I buy and who I support. Interstingly just writing this was really easy as it's something that has been on my mind.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I found the Siberian method very interesting. Since I have read about it I have tried to practice it several times. I think my wife finds it quite strange when I stop and start taking breaths instead of arguing back. I think it will require a lot of practice but it is great to notice the anger building up and choosing not to act on it. It then dissipates very quickly
